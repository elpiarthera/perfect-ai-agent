## Defects caught that the review passes missed

The dedicated review is a signal, not a verdict. Independent verification found more
than the reviews did:

1. **Narrator gender.** Both FR drafts marked the narrator feminine ("je suis allee",
   "je me suis souvenue"). Checked against the corpus: all 133 published FR entries
   leave the narrator's participles unmarked. Recast to unmark - not switched to
   masculine, not harmonised to feminine. A reviewer explicitly demanded consistent
   feminine; that was rejected because the corpus says otherwise.
2. **Dropped paragraph (Day 134).** The closing paragraph of the fifth section was
   merged into the previous one. Paragraph alignment EN/FR exposed it: 26 vs 25.
   Restored; parity now 26/26 and 33/33.
3. **"c'est son appel"** for "it is his call" - contresens. "Call" here is a decision to
   make, not a phone call. Now "c'est a lui de trancher".
4. **"une nouvelle sur la charte"** - the noun was missing, so it read as "a piece of
   news". English is "a new one" = a new lock.
5. **Seven residual calques** found on a full read-aloud after both reviews had passed,
   including "qui est comment tu ecris une serrure" (word-for-word English), a
   "ne...pas...aucun" double negative, and three missing prepositions.
