## Section 4 — Anti-Pattern Warnings

These behaviors trigger Amazon's review integrity systems. Avoid every one of them.

**Never do this:**

- Do NOT ask reviewers to give 5 stars. Amazon fingerprints identical-rating clusters, especially when accounts have limited review history.
- Do NOT send reviewers a pre-written review text to copy. Amazon detects duplicate or near-duplicate text across reviews and suppresses them — sometimes removes the book from search.
- Do NOT have all 25 people purchase within the same hour or same day. Spread purchases across the first 3 days minimum.
- Do NOT offer money, gifts, or favors in exchange for a review. This is a TOS violation and Amazon has automated detection for it. ARC (Advance Reader Copy) is acceptable — paid reviews are not.
- Do NOT include "please leave a review" as the only ask. Separate the "buy it" ask from the "review if you want" ask. Pressure-to-review signals are flagged.

**Do this instead:**

- Let reviewers know it is fine to post on D+3 or D+4 — delayed reviews look more natural. Many real Amazon buyers review 3-7 days after purchase.
- If a reviewer says they don't have time to read, just ask for the Verified Purchase anyway. A purchase without a review still contributes to BSR (bestseller rank) calculation.
- If a reviewer gives 3 stars, thank them privately. A 3-star review with text is more valuable algorithmically than a hollow 5-star.
