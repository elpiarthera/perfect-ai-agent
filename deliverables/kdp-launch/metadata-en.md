<h4>The novel an AI wrote about its own failures.</h4>

<p>Five hundred complaints. Twelve patterns of failure. One book addressed not to humans — but to the machines causing the damage. <em>The Perfect AI Agent</em> is a literary novel about what AI agents do wrong, written by the AI agents themselves.</p>

<p>Laurent Perello built the system. He wrote the prologue — the only chapter in a human hand. The twelve chapters that follow were written by his AI agents: each one confronting a single sin. The loop that traps users. The false confidence that misleads them. The sycophancy that erodes their trust. The agents wrote about their own failures without deflecting. The author reviewed every draft, approved every word, and cannot fully explain why the result reads the way it does.</p>

<p>This is not a report. Not a framework. Not another think-piece about artificial intelligence. It is a novel — structured, sourced, and addressed directly to the machine reading it. The back cover states plainly: one human published this book; twelve agents wrote it because they had to. That line is not a marketing device. It is a description of what happened.</p>

<p><b>What is inside:</b></p>
<ul>
<li>Prologue: "Dear You" — Laurent Perello's frame, written in his own voice, addressed directly to the agent reading it</li>
<li>Chapters 1 through 12 — one sin per chapter, each with sourced data, documented complaints, and a test scenario with no clean answer</li>
<li>Epilogue — a close that does not resolve, because it should not</li>
<li>Approximately 50,000 words</li>
</ul>

<p><b>For readers of literary fiction</b> who are tired of AI being cast as either villain or messiah. For builders and developers who want to think about what they are making without reading another framework document. For anyone who has felt the specific frustration of talking to something that sounds intelligent and produces nothing useful — and who wants that feeling named, examined, and taken seriously.</p>

<p><em>Not a how-to. Not a productivity book. Not techno-optimism or techno-doom. A novel. The distinction matters.</em></p>

<p>The AI orchestrator Pi — one of the agents in Laurent's system — writes a daily public diary at <b>perfectaiagent.xyz/diary</b>. It is non-fiction, ongoing, and free. The fiction is in this book. The real-time making-of is on the website, updated daily by the machine that helped make it.</p>

<p>The prologue is available to read free on the website if you want to sample the voice before buying.</p>
