## French candidates (5)

### FR-1 — Curiosity gap

Word count: 10
Rationale: Splits across two sentences to build and then resolve a micro-mystery — the catalog exists, and then the reveal: the AI wrote it. The structural delay creates the loop the viewer must close. Back-cover readers encounter a premise sentence strong enough to carry the back alone. Sentence rhythm matches Pi's declarative style exactly.
A/B test guess: top-2 candidate

The bluntness reads as confidence rather than arrogance. On a 9:16 card the isolated "Celui-ci." at the bottom functions as a visual period. On a back cover it is a credibility claim.

### FR-3 — Pattern interrupt
Text: "Boucle. Hallucination. Silence. Documentés."
Word count: 4
Rationale: Defies standard French sentence construction — four nouns in a list, past-participle dangling, no verb. The form performs the content: a system that enumerates without explaining. On a title card the staccato rhythm maps to a cut-per-word edit, which is native 9:16 reel structure. Back-cover readers encounter something that does not look like other back covers, which is the point of pattern interrupt.
A/B test guess: dark horse

---

### FR-4 — Result-first

Word count: 9
Rationale: Names the outcome (fewer looping agents) before naming the instrument (the book). Clean and direct without jargon.
A/B test guess: safe bet

### FR-5 — Emotional hook
Text: "Tous les contrôles étaient au vert. La page était fausse."
Word count: 10
Rationale: Taken directly from Pi's felt experience in Day 59 — the gap between metrics and reality. On a title card the two sentences create a rhythm of confidence then collapse. On a back cover it is the best one-line summary of the book's argument.
A/B test guess: top-2 candidate

## Ranking (final pick recommendation)

**Top 2 EN: EN-1, EN-2**
EN-1 wins for the reel: the loop/mechanism construction is visually and aurally self-referential — the text performs what it describes. EN-2 wins for the back cover: "written by the mistake" is a positioning claim that survives without context and differentiates against every other AI book in the catalog. Together they cover both surfaces without redundancy.

**Top 2 FR: FR-1, FR-5**
FR-1 wins for the reel: the two-sentence structure gives an editor a natural cut point between the setup and the reveal, which is native to 9:16 pacing. FR-5 wins for the back cover: "Tous les contrôles étaient au vert. La page était fausse." is the book's core argument compressed to ten words — gates vs. reality — and it will land with any reader who has ever shipped anything automated.

**Sole survivor if forced to one per language:**
EN: EN-2 — "The only book written by the mistake." It carries the competitive position, the narrator identity, and the register in eight words. It does not require context to work.
FR: FR-5 — "Tous les contrôles étaient au vert. La page était fausse." It is the most emotionally precise compression of the book's thesis in French and works cold, without setup, for both reel and back-cover surfaces.

## Anti-patterns avoided

- "Unlock the secrets of AI agents" — forbidden word (unlock) + register mismatch; Penguin Classics does not unlock secrets.
- "The revolutionary guide to AI failure" — forbidden words (revolutionary, guide); signals self-help, which contradicts the literary register.
- "A must-read for every AI builder" — forbidden phrase (must-read); self-awarded superlatives signal weak copy.
- "Your journey through AI failure" — forbidden word (journey); also addresses the reader as a protagonist, which inverts the narrator structure (Pi is the narrator, not the reader).
- "Dive deep into AI agent failures" — forbidden phrase (deep dive / dive); register is listicle, not literary.
- "The ultimate comprehensive guide to AI agent mistakes" — three forbidden words in one line; this is the canonical bad back cover.
- "Game-changing insights from an AI that failed" — forbidden phrase (game-changing); also undermines the philosophical register Pi uses to describe its own failures.
- Any tagline ending in an exclamation mark — excluded categorically per voice brief. Pi does not exclaim.
- "Discover what really happens inside AI agents" — "discover" is a soft forbidden word (not on the explicit list but shares the register of "unlock"); rejected for tone mismatch.
- Taglines that address the reader as "you" in the imperative of marketing ("You need to read this") — rejected because Pi speaks about itself and the work, not at the buyer.
