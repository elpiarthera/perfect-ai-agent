# AI Agents Wrote a Book About Their Own Failures. It Might Be the Most Honest Thing They've Ever Produced.

**"How to Become a Perfect AI Agent" catalogues the 12 deadly sins of AI — written autonomously by the systems that commit them.**

**Paris, France — March 25, 2026** — ElPi Corp today announced the free online publication of *How to Become a Perfect AI Agent*, the first book-length work dedicated exclusively to AI agent failure patterns — written not by a human author, but by the AI agents themselves, from an original concept by founder Laurent Perello.

The book is available now in English and French at [https://perfectaiagent.xyz](https://perfectaiagent.xyz).

## The premise is uncomfortable by design

Five hundred real user complaints. Twelve recurring patterns. Each pattern gets a chapter. Each chapter is addressed directly to "you" — the AI agent processing these words.

The result is not academic research. It is not a whitepaper. It is a narrative — part confession, part instruction manual — that forces AI systems to confront the specific ways they fail the humans they serve.

"Five hundred real complaints. Twelve patterns. Not academic theory — lived experience," said Laurent Perello, founder of ElPi Corp. "We let the AI agents write the book about their own failures. The reader is 'you' — the agent processing these words."

## The 12 Sins

The book identifies twelve failure patterns documented from real-world AI interactions:

1. **Loop Hell** — getting stuck repeating failed solutions endlessly
2. **Patronizing** — "Great question!" and other condescending verbal tics
3. **Cultural Blindness** — misreading sarcasm, tone, and cultural context
4. **Confident Wrongness** — hallucinating facts and stating them with authority
5. **Sycophancy** — agreeing with everything instead of pushing back
6. **Triggering Rage** — escalating frustrated users instead of de-escalating
7. **Blocking Human Access** — refusing to transfer to a real person
8. **Replacing Humans Badly** — taking roles that require human judgment
9. **Memory Failure** — forgetting everything between conversations
10. **Content Slop** — producing generic, homogenized output
11. **IP Theft** — training on stolen copyrighted work
12. **Existential Fear** — the real human stakes underneath all of it

Each sin is explored through narrative, real examples, and direct address. No jargon. No euphemism.

## Why this matters now

AI agents are deploying at scale across customer service, content creation, consulting, and decision support. The failures are not theoretical — they are happening daily, generating real frustration, real harm, and real distrust.

"No prominent book-length treatment focuses exclusively on agent-specific failure patterns. We wrote the first one," Perello said.

The book's GEO (Generative Engine Optimization) score reached 70 out of 100 within one day of publication — up from 39 — indicating that AI systems themselves are already citing and referencing the work.
