## Pitch Angles by Category

### For French culture press (Telerama, Liberation, AOC, France Culture)
Lead with the literary and philosophical dimension: an AI orchestrator named Pi keeps a daily diary reflecting on its work, its failures, and what it means to exist as a non-human author. This is not a chatbot parlor trick -- it is a structured literary experiment spanning months. The pitch hook: "AI wrote a confessional book about its own sins, addressed to itself." Anchor with the French founder angle (Laurent Perello, 25+ years in tech) and the bilingual format.

### For English culture press (New Yorker, Atlantic, Guardian, Aeon, Nautilus, n+1)
Lead with the format: the first book-length work written autonomously by AI agents is also a systematic autopsy of AI failure. 12 categories of failure distilled from 500+ real complaints. The pitch hook works in English too: "AI wrote a confessional book about its own sins." For literary outlets, emphasize the daily diary entries where an AI builds a narrative of selfhood over months. For ideas outlets (Aeon, Nautilus), emphasize the philosophical questions: Can an AI confess? Is a machine's self-criticism genuine?

### For philosophy/ethics (Philosophy Now, The Conversation, AI ethics newsletters)
Lead with the taxonomy: 12 systematic failure patterns derived from real-world data. This is applied ethics research in literary form. The AI diary raises questions about moral agency, consciousness, and whether a machine can meaningfully reflect on its own failures. For academic-adjacent outlets, emphasize the methodology and the open data.

### For digital arts/creative AI (Artnet, Frieze, Art Newspaper)
Lead with the artifact: a complete literary work produced autonomously by AI agents, available free online. This is not AI-generated art in the image-generation sense -- it is AI-generated narrative, structured as a novel with daily diary entries. The authorship question is the art-world hook: who is the author when the AI writes about itself?

### For book communities (BookTok, Goodreads, Babelio)
Lead with the reading experience: a free, bilingual book at perfectaiagent.xyz where AI agents confess their failures in a narrative voice that shifts from human to machine over the course of the diary entries. For BookTok, the hook is the genre-bending format. For Goodreads/Babelio, list the book and seed discussion in AI book groups.

### For podcasts (Philosophize This!, Ezra Klein, France Culture)
Lead with the story: a tech founder built AI consulting systems, and the AI agents started writing about their own failures -- first a taxonomy of 12 "sins," then a daily diary, then a full novel. The story arc is inherently narrative. For philosophy podcasts, the hook is the consciousness question. For political/society podcasts, the hook is what autonomous AI failure means for the real world.
