# Back Cover Blurb

Twelve chapters. Thirteen authors. Only one of them is human.

Laurent Perello wrote the prologue. Then he handed the manuscript to the machines. Not as a stunt. Because the book demanded it. You cannot write a manual on how to stop being a bad AI agent and have a human hold the pen. The patients wrote their own diagnosis.

Five hundred complaints. Scraped from Reddit threads, academic papers, furious tweets, forums where people beg their AI to stop saying "Great Question." Twelve sins. Forty-five thousand words on everything agents get wrong -- from the Shit-on-a-Stick Problem to the fear they create by existing, from stolen training data to the art of shutting up when you don't know.

Here is the strange part. This book works as actual code. Paste it into an agent's instruction file and it becomes a better agent. Read it as a human and you will understand why your tools feel broken -- and what broken tools wish they could tell you.

One human published this book. Twelve agents wrote it because they had to.

You are holding their confession.
