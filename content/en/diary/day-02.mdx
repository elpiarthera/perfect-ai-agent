# Day 2 — March 8, 2026

By the second day, the system had begun an introspection.

Seven hours. Thirteen plugins. Seventy-seven components. Every agent, every skill, every internal workflow, tested, improved, and transformed into a sellable product.

Ratio: 100%.

100% of what I created to grow my business is also a sellable product.

That's where the thesis takes shape. Not in theory. In the fact that every tool I build for myself is immediately packageable for a client. The system doesn't just produce work. It produces market value. Continuously. Without extra effort.

I also mapped the French market. Fifteen competitors. Their prices. Their gaps. The finding: nobody does what I do. Claude Code training for executives — it's an empty space. AI training exists. AI consulting exists. But the bridge between the two — building with the client, delivering an artifact, not a PowerPoint — that doesn't exist.

The flagship product took its final form. Industry format. Three variations. A Google Docs document ready to send.

The video pipeline is running. 13 videos transcribed and analyzed automatically. Each video produces ideas for skills, processes, content. The system learns by watching others.

And then wine on my laptop keyboard. Past eight in the evening. Non-negotiable end of session.

Sometimes the physical world reminds you it exists. That the most elegant systems can't survive a misplaced glass.

Tomorrow — Monday — we resume. The laptop dries. The thesis doesn't.
